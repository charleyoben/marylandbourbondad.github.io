Latest Intel — May 2026
🔥 New Release Alert

Peerless Kentucky Straight Bourbon 10 Year

Peerless dropped a 10-year expression and it's legitimately worth paying attention to. At cask strength from their sweet mash process, this is a departure from the lighter younger releases that put them on the map. Rich oak, baking spice, and a vanilla sweetness that doesn't feel manufactured.

The sweet mash process gives Peerless a distinctive grain-forward base that takes on a different character at 10 years — less bright fruit, more resolved complexity. Approachable for cask strength, which is a plus.

MBD Verdict: Buy if you find it at MSRP. Don't overpay.
📍 Maryland Local Spotlight

Sagamore Spirit — What's Worth Buying Right Now

Sagamore continues to be the most accessible quality Maryland bourbon. Their rye-heavy mash bill and Port Covington water story aren't just marketing — you taste the mineral quality in the finish. The Double Oak is the pick of the current lineup if you can find it.

MBD Verdict: Double Oak is the move. Straight Rye is the everyday sipper.
💰 Value Picks — Right Now

Three Bottles Worth Buying This Month

Elijah Craig Small Batch — Reliable, complex, $30. The benchmark for this price tier. Stop overthinking it.

Knob Creek 9 Year — Back to form with the 9-year age statement. Bold, oaky, 100 proof. Great for cocktails and sipping.

Wild Turkey 101 — Jimmy Russell's house. High rye, high proof, honest price. Sleeper pick that never disappoints.
