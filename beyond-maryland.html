Beyond Maryland

When the hunt takes me outside state lines — Kentucky, Tennessee, and everywhere the good stuff lives.

Maryland pride is real, but the bourbon world doesn't stop at the state line. This section covers distillery visits, bottle finds, and regional intel from trips beyond the Old Line State. Mostly Kentucky, occasionally further.

Kentucky Trail Notes

Distillery visits, bourbon trail intel, and what's actually worth the stop.

Kentucky · Bardstown

Bardstown Bourbon Company

The collaborative distillery model here is genuinely interesting. Multiple producer relationships and a serious food program make this more than a standard tour. The Fusion series is worth tracking.

Kentucky · Loretto

Maker's Mark

The campus is beautiful and the tour is well-produced. The Private Select program at the gift shop is the real draw if you want something exclusive. Budget extra time for the gift shop.

Kentucky · Lawrenceburg

Four Roses

The Spanish Mission architecture alone makes it worth the stop. More importantly: the Limited Small Batch releases are often available in the tasting room that you won't find at retail. Show up with a list.

Kentucky · Louisville

Angel's Envy

Urban distillery in the Nulu neighborhood, walkable from good restaurants. The finishing program is on full display here. If the Triple Oak is in the tasting room, taste it before you buy anywhere else — context helps.

Retail Finds Outside Maryland

States with better allocation, better prices, or just better luck.

Virginia ABC stores punch above their weight for allocated bottles. Pennsylvania Fine Wine stores have inconsistent selection but occasionally surprising finds. Delaware has favorable pricing on everyday bottles. New Jersey Total Wine locations see better Buffalo Trace allocation than Maryland — worth the trip for a haul.

"The best bourbon find of last year was in a small Virginia ABC 40 minutes from home. Go where the hunters aren't."
