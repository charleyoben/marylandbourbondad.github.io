Nose

Dark caramel, roasted oak, vanilla extract, and a whisper of dried cherry. The proof announces itself immediately — big but controlled. Caramelized sugar with underlying baking spice.

Palate

Dense and oily. Vanilla leads, followed by dark fruit and a building heat. Baking spice mid-palate with hints of toasted walnut and leather. The mouthfeel is exceptional for cask strength.

Finish

Long and warm. Oak tannins that aren't harsh, transitioning to a sweet vanilla and dried fruit note that lingers for a solid minute. No burn for the proof.

Water Notes

A few drops opens the nose considerably — more fruit comes through, some floral notes emerge. Both neat and with water are valid approaches.

MBD Take

Best Booker's batch in recent memory. If you see it at MSRP, buy it. Don't pay secondary. At around $90 this is exceptional value for a true cask strength expression with this level of complexity.

Nose

Toffee and dried cherry upfront. The port cask influence is present but not overwhelming. Subtle earthiness that develops into something almost chocolatey with time in the glass.

Palate

Rich and layered. The triple finish adds complexity without muddying the profile. Dark fruit, baking spice, and a pronounced vanilla that feels earned rather than added. Softer mouthfeel than the proof suggests.

Finish

Medium-long. Fruit notes fade slowly, leaving a pleasant oak and spice note. Cleaner finish than the standard AE — the additional finishing seems to have resolved some of the rougher edges.

Value Assessment

$70 is fair for what you're getting. Not a steal, not a rip-off. The standard AE port finish is very good; this is better. The difference is meaningful enough to justify the price premium.

MBD Take

Skeptics of the finishing trend should taste this before writing it off. This is what the format looks like when it works — the three-cask process adds genuine complexity rather than just sweetness.
