Maryland Bourbon Dad

The Distillery Scout

Maryland distilleries, reviewed and mapped. Old Line State pride — we make good bourbon here.

Maryland has a real distilling tradition — rye whiskey was a Maryland thing long before Kentucky took the crown. The modern craft scene is young but growing fast. Here's where I've been and what I think.

Sagamore Spirit

MBD: Must Visit
📍 Port Covington, Baltimore, MD

The most polished distillery experience in Maryland by a significant margin. The facility at Port Covington is genuinely impressive — massive rickhouses, a well-run tasting room, and guides who know their material. The water story (Maryland limestone-filtered spring water) isn't just marketing.

The product lineup is solid and improving. The Straight Rye is the baseline; the Double Oak and cask-strength expressions are where it gets interesting. Tour prices are fair and the tasting is generous.

What to Buy

  • 🥇 Double Oak Rye — the flagship expression
  • 🥈 Straight Rye — everyday sipper, priced right
  • 🔍 Limited Cask Strength — grab it when you see it

Old Line Spirits

MBD: Worth the Drive
📍 Baltimore, MD

Old Line does things a little differently — they focus on American Single Malt, which puts them in a less-crowded category and lets their grain work speak without the bourbon reference points. The tasting room in Baltimore is casual and genuinely fun, not corporate.

The rum lineup has improved significantly over the last two years. If you haven't been back recently, it's worth another visit.

What to Buy

  • 🥇 American Single Malt — the standout
  • 🥈 Aged Rum — better than you'd expect

Tenth Ward Distilling

MBD: Local Gem
📍 Frederick, MD

Frederick's craft scene is underrated across the board, and Tenth Ward is part of why. Smaller operation, more experimental, and more willing to try things that don't fit a standard category. The absinthe alone is worth the trip if you're into that.

What to Try

  • 🥇 Maryland Straight Rye Whiskey
  • 🔍 Experimental releases — ask what's in the tasting room
